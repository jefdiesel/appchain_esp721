Just Fucking Use Ethscriptions

Arbitrary data storage on Ethereum L1. Forever. For pennies.

What is this?

Every Ethereum transaction has a calldata field. It's meant for smart contract inputs, but here's the thing: you can put whatever the fuck you want in there. The protocol doesn't care. It's just bytes.

Ethscriptions exploit this. Take any data — literally any data — encode it as hex, and shove it in calldata. Send the transaction to yourself. Congratulations, your data now lives on Ethereum mainnet. Not a sidechain. Not a rollup. The actual L1.

It's arbitrary data storage

This isn't limited to images or text. It's arbitrary bytes. Anything that can be represented as data can be inscribed:

  • Images — PNG, JPEG, SVG, GIF, whatever
  • Text — UTF-8, ASCII, any encoding
  • JSON — Configs, metadata, structured data
  • HTML/CSS/JS — Full web pages, bundled apps
  • Binary — Compiled code, archives, encrypted blobs
  • Anything — If it's bytes, it goes on-chain

The only limit is the block gas limit, which practically caps you around 100KB per transaction. Need more? Split it across multiple transactions and reassemble.

It's stupid cheap

Calldata costs 16 gas per non-zero byte, 4 gas per zero byte. At 10 gwei and $3500 ETH, that's roughly $0.03 per kilobyte.

A 10KB image? Thirty cents. A 50KB bundled web app? A buck fifty. One time. Forever. Not per month. Not per year. One transaction, permanent storage.

Compare that to S3 egress fees, Pinata subscriptions, or whatever the fuck Filecoin is doing this week.

It's actually permanent

Ethereum has processed every block since July 2015. Not a single byte of calldata has ever been lost. Every full node stores it. Every archive node stores it. Block explorers index it. The data is replicated across thousands of independent machines worldwide.

This isn't "permanent until the company pivots." This isn't "permanent until funding runs out." This is permanent because the protocol requires it. Nodes that don't store the full chain can't validate it. The data stays because Ethereum fundamentally cannot work without it.

Nobody can delete it

There's no admin panel. No support ticket. No "report this content" button. The Ethereum protocol has exactly one way to modify historical data: mass coordinated agreement from the entire network to hard fork it out.

That's happened exactly once (The DAO hack) and caused a permanent chain split. Nobody's doing that for your data. Your transaction is final.

But IPFS/Arweave/Filecoin...

IPFS is an addressing system, not storage. If nobody pins your content, it disappears. "Content-addressed" doesn't mean "content-stored."

Arweave bets on tokenomics incentivizing permanent storage. It's a 6-year-old experiment. Ethereum has 10 years of uptime and secures $400+ billion in value.

Filecoin requires active storage deals with payments. Miss a payment? Data's gone. Provider goes offline? Hope you had redundancy.

Ethscriptions require nothing after the initial transaction. No ongoing payments. No deal renewals. No trust in future tokenomics. It's done.

Use cases

Anything where permanence matters more than size:

  • On-chain NFT media — Actual images, not IPFS pointers
  • Immutable configs — Protocol parameters, governance records
  • Proof of existence — Timestamps for documents, hashes, claims
  • Censorship-resistant publishing — Content no platform can remove
  • Permanent archives — Historical records, cultural artifacts
  • Trustless data availability — Reference data for L2s and apps

How it works

1. Take your data
2. Encode as data URI: data:mime/type;base64,...
3. Convert that string to hex
4. Send a transaction with that hex as calldata
5. Done. It's on Ethereum forever.

The transaction hash is now a permanent pointer to your data. Anyone can read it from any Ethereum node, any block explorer, any RPC endpoint. The data is the transaction. The transaction is the data.

Stop trusting third parties to keep your data alive. Stop paying rent on storage. Stop accepting "permanent*" with asterisks.
